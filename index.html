Naming Conventions

  • Direction Reference: Directions are specified as if looking into a mirror:
    • Straight → The same side, as in the mirror.
    • Cross → The opposite side, as in the mirror.
  • Leg Positioning:
    • Inside → The attacker's legs are between the opponent's legs.
    • Outside → The attacker's legs are next to the hip of the attacked leg.

Catching the Heel

Reap the knee using your legs and/or hands.

Simply rotate to follow the hidden heel.

Play between a straight ankle lock, a heel hook, and an aoki lock.

Breaking Mechanics

Taking Out the Slack

  1. Control the hip:
    • Pull their hip towards you using your legs.
    • If they are not on the floor, weigh their leg down to immobilize the hip.
  2. Control the heel:
    • Pull the heel towards your head using your arms.

Finishing

Belly Up: Rotate your knees to one side while rotating your shoulders to the other, while crunching their toes to their butt.

Belly Down: Put weight on the heel to immobilize it and finish with a side knee-bar.

Defending the Heel Hook

Diminishing Foot Control

Hiding the Heel:

  • Unreap your knee.
  • Point your toes and glue your attacked foot to their body, the floor, or your other foot.

Weight your foot and come up if possible.

Slipping the Heel (once they catch it):

  • Same movement as hiding the heel.
  • Applying forward pressure might help, but be careful.

Diminishing Hip Control

Separate their legs and/or pummel your legs to reduce their control over your hip and/or knee, or better, gain control of their hip to reverse the position.

Finishing with Both Legs

Separate their legs while maintaining control as much as possible.

Common Problems & Solutions

Inside Straight (Controlling Rotation)

However, you have a dilemma of heel exposure vs double leg control.

Solution:

  • Control both legs or
  • Use breaking mechanics to control rotation:
    • Belly down finish in the same entanglement.

  • Transition to an outside straight entanglement while rolling.

Outside Cross (Hand Fighting Defense)

Problem: The opponent can use their hands to free the heel.

Solution: Place your legs between you and them, which means you have to use a belly-down finish.

Inside Cross (Controlling Rotation)

Problem: The opponent can hide the heel very well, rolling when needed.

Solution:

  • Control both legs or
  • Transition to straight inside entanglement.

Outside Straight (Back Exposure Risk)

Problem: They have access to your torso, which means they can use it to gain top position and sprawl their leg free, or take your back.

Solution:

  • Face them and keep your knee between you
  • Possibly change into inside straight, or put your outside knee between their legs
  • When they are down, belly down to dig for their heel, making sure to weight their hip so that they can't come on top or use an Achilles grip.
  • Here you can also sit on them and take double leg control
